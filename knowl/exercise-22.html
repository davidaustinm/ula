Exercise 1.

Consider the vectors
\begin{equation*} \vvec = \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right], \wvec = \left[\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
  1. Sketch these vectors below.
  2. Compute the vectors \(-3\vvec\text{,}\) \(2\wvec\text{,}\) \(\vvec + \wvec\text{,}\) and \(\vvec - \wvec\) and add them into the sketch above.
  3. Sketch below the set of vectors having the form \(2\vvec + c\wvec\) where \(c\) is any scalar.
  4. Sketch below the line \(y=3x - 2\text{.}\) Then identify two vectors \(\vvec\) and \(\wvec\) so that this line is described by \(\vvec + c\wvec\text{.}\) Are there other choices for the vectors \(\vvec\) and \(\wvec\text{?}\)
