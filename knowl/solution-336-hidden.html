Applying Gram-Schmidt, we have \(\wvec_1=\threevec4{-2}4\) and \(\wvec_2=\threevec360\text{,}\) which leads to \(\uvec_1=\threevec{2/3}{-1/3}{2/3}\) and \(\uvec_2=\threevec{1/\sqrt{5}}{2/\sqrt{5}}0\text{.}\) It therefore follows that \(\vvec_1 = 6\uvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2 = 6\uvec_1 + 3\sqrt{5}\uvec_2\text{.}\)
This leads to \(A=QR\) where \(Q=\begin{bmatrix} 2/3 \amp 1/\sqrt{5} \\ -1/3 \amp 2/\sqrt{5} \\ 2/3 \amp 0 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(R = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \amp 6 \\ 0 \amp 3\sqrt{5} \end{bmatrix} \text{.}\)
