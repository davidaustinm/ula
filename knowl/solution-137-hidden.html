1. The vectors are linearly independent and span \(\real^2\text{.}\) We can see this by forming the matrix
    \begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{rr} \vvec_1 \amp \vvec_2 \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rr} -2 \amp 2 \\ 2 \amp 1 \\ \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 1 \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. The grid on the figure indicates that
    1. \(\xvec = \twovec{-6}{3}\text{.}\)
    2. \(\xvec = \twovec{-2}{-4}\text{.}\)
    3. \(\xvec = \twovec63\text{.}\)
  3. The grid on the figure indicates that
    1. \(\coords{\xvec}{\bcal} = \twovec{0}{-1}\text{.}\)
    2. \(\coords{\xvec}{\bcal} = \twovec{1}{2}\text{.}\)
    3. \(\coords{\xvec}{\bcal} = \twovec{-2}{-1}\text{.}\)
  4. We form the matrix
    \begin{equation*} P_{\bcal}=\left[\begin{array}{rr} \vvec_1 \amp \vvec_2 \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rr} -2 \amp 2 \\ 2 \amp 1 \\ \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
    so that \(\xvec=P_{\bcal}\coords{\xvec}{\bcal}\text{.}\) We then need to solve the equation \(P_{\bcal}\coords{\xvec}{\bcal} = \twovec{60}{90}\text{,}\) which gives \(\coords{\xvec}{\bcal} = \twovec{20}{50}\text{.}\)
