Of the bicycles that begin at \(P\text{,}\) 60% of them end up at \(P\text{.}\) Therefore \(0.6x_1\) is the number of bicycles that begin at \(P\) and end at \(P\text{.}\) Similarly, 70% of the bicycles that begin at \(Q\) end up at \(P\) so this number is \(0.7x_2\text{.}\) Therefore, the total number of bicycles that end up at \(P\) is
    \begin{equation*} 0.6x_1 + 0.7x_2. \end{equation*}
  2. In the same way, the number of bicycles that end up at \(Q\) is
    \begin{equation*} 0.4x_1 + 0.3x_2. \end{equation*}
  3. This gives the matrix
    \begin{equation*} T(\xvec) = \twovec{0.6x_1+0.7x_2}{0.4x_1+0.3x_2} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.6 \amp 0.7 \\ 0.4 \amp 0.3 \\ \end{bmatrix} \xvec. \end{equation*}
  4. This expression leads to \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 0.6 \amp 0.7 \\ 0.4 \amp 0.3 \\ \end{bmatrix} \text{.}\)
