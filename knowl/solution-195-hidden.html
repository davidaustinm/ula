  2. With this assumption, \(A\vvec = 0\vvec = \zerovec\text{,}\) which means that \(\vvec\) is a solution to the homogeneous equation \(A\xvec = \zerovec\text{.}\)
  3. The column space of \(A\) is spanned by \(\vvec_1=\threevec{1}{1}{1}\) and we notice that \(A\vvec_1 = 3\vvec_1\text{.}\) Therefore, \(\vvec_1\) is an eigenvector with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda_1=3\text{.}\)
    We also know that that the rank of this matrix is 1 so \(\nul(A)\) is two-dimensional. A basis for the null space is \(\vvec_2=\threevec{-1}{1}{0}\) and \(\vvec_3=\threevec{-1}{0}{1}\) so these vectors are eigenvectors with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda_2=\lambda_3 = 0\text{.}\) In the same way, \(\vvec_1=\threevec{1}{2}{3}\) is an eigenvector of \(B\) with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda_1=6\) and \(\vvec_2=\threevec{-1}{1}{0}\) and \(\vvec_3=\threevec{-1}{0}{1}\) are eigenvectors with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda_2=\lambda_3=0\text{.}\)
