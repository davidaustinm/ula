If \(A\) is a symmetric matrix, then the quadratic form defined by \(A\) is the function \(q_A(\xvec) = \xvec\cdot(A\xvec)\text{.}\) Quadratic forms appear when studying the variance of a dataset. If \(C\) is the covariance matrix, then the variance in the direction defined by a unit vector \(\uvec\) is \(q_C(\uvec) = \uvec\cdot(C\uvec)=V_{\uvec}\text{.}\)
