Item e.
The eigenvalues are \(\lambda_1=1\text{,}\) which has multiplicity two, and \(\lambda_2=-1\text{.}\) The eigenspace \(E_1\) is two-dimensional and spanned by the probability vectors \(\qvec_1=\threevec{\frac13}{\frac23}0\) and \(\qvec_2=\threevec001\text{.}\) Both of these vectors are steady-state vectors so there is not a unique steady-state vector.
