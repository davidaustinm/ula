Item 3.2.5.4.b.
From the reduced row echelon form, we see that the set of vectors spans \(\real^3\) because there is a pivot position in every row. We also see that \(\vvec_3 = -2\vvec_1+\vvec_2\) and \(\vvec_5 = -\vvec_1-\vvec_4\text{.}\) This means that \(\vvec_1\text{,}\) \(\vvec_2\text{,}\) and \(\vvec_4\) will span \(\real^3\) and therefore form a basis.
