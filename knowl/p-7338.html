Remember that \(A=\begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 2 \\ 2 \amp 1 \end{bmatrix}\) is symmetric so that \(A=QDQ^T\) where \(D\) is the diagonal matrix above and \(Q\) is the orthogonal matrix that rotates vectors by \(45^\circ\text{.}\) Notice that
\begin{equation*} q_A(\uvec) = \uvec\cdot(A\uvec) = \uvec\cdot(QDQ^T\uvec) = (Q^T\uvec)\cdot(DQ^T\uvec) = q_D(\vvec) \end{equation*}
where \(\vvec=Q^T\uvec\text{.}\) That is, we have \(q_A(\uvec) = q_D(\vvec)\text{.}\)
