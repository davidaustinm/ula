If we are given a vector \(\bvec\) in \(\real^3\text{,}\) we can find a vector \(\yvec\) such that \(A\yvec = \bvec\) since the columns of \(A\) span \(\real^3\text{.}\) Since the columns of \(B\) span \(\real^4\text{,}\) we can find a vector \(\xvec\) such that \(B\xvec=\yvec\text{.}\) Then \(AB\xvec = \bvec\text{,}\) which means that \(B\) is the span of the columns of \(AB\text{.}\)
