1. We have \(T(\xvec) = A\xvec = \zerovec\text{.}\) The reduced row echelon form of \(A\) is \(A \sim \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1 \amp 0 \amp -2 \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp 1 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 0 \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.}\) Therefore, the solutions have the form \(\xvec=x_3\threevec{2}{-1}{1}\text{.}\)
  2. We see that
    \begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{rrr|r} 1 \amp 3 \amp 1 \amp -8 \\ -2 \amp 1 \amp 5 \amp 9 \\ 0 \amp 2 \amp 2 \amp 2 \\ \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{rrr|r} 1 \amp 0 \amp -2 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp 1 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 0 \amp 1 \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}
    Since this is an inconsistent system, there are no vectors \(\xvec\) satisfying the equation.
  3. In the same way, we have
    \begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{rrr|r} 1 \amp 3 \amp 1 \amp -8 \\ -2 \amp 1 \amp 5 \amp 2 \\ 0 \amp 2 \amp 2 \amp -4 \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{rrrr} 1 \amp 0 \amp -2 \amp -2 \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp 1 \amp -2 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 0 \amp 0 \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}
    This shows that \(\xvec=\threevec{-2}{-2}{0} + x_3\threevec{2}{-1}{1}\text{.}\)
