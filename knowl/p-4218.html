If \(A=PDP^{-1}\text{,}\)
  1. \(A\) is invertible since \(\det A = \det D = -3\text{.}\)
  2. We know that \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\) are eigenvectors of \(A\) with associated eigenvalues \(\lambda_1=3\) and \(\lambda_2=-1\text{.}\) If \(\vvec\) is an eigenvector of \(A\) with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda\text{,}\) then \(\vvec\) is an eigenvector of \(A^{-1}\) with associated eigenvalue \(\frac1{\lambda}\text{.}\) Therefore, \(A^{-1} = PEP^{-1}\) where \(E = \mattwo{\frac13}00{-1}\text{.}\)
  3. We have \(A^3=PFP^{-1}\) where \(F=\mattwo{27}00{-1}\text{.}\)
