1. We will equate the number of hydrogen and oxygen atoms before and after the reaction.
    1. Before the reaction, there are two hydrogen atoms for every hydrogen molecule. Since there are \(x\) hydrogen molecules, we have \(2x\) hydrogen atoms before the reaction.
      After the reaction, there are two hydrogen atoms for every water molecule. Since there are \(z\) hydrogen molecules, we have \(2z\) hydrogen atoms after the reaction. This gives the equation \(2x=2z\text{.}\)
    2. Similarly, there are \(2y\) oxygen atoms before the reaction and \(z\) oxygen atoms after. This gives the equation \(2y = z\text{.}\)
    3. We have the linear system
      \begin{equation*} \begin{alignedat}{4} 2x \amp \amp \amp {}-{} \amp 2z \amp {}={} \amp 0 \\ \amp \amp 2y \amp {}-{} \amp z \amp {}={} \amp 0\text{.} \\ \end{alignedat} \end{equation*}
      When we create the augmented matrix and find its reduced row echelon form, we see that \(z\) is a free variable so that
      \begin{equation*} \begin{alignedat}{2} x \amp {}={} \amp z \\ y \amp {}={} \amp \frac12 z\text{.} \\ \end{alignedat} \end{equation*}
      We need all the quantities to be integers, and the smallest value of \(z\) that makes \(y\) an integer is \(z=2\text{.}\) Therefore, we have \(x=2\text{,}\) \(y=1\text{,}\) and \(z=2\text{.}\) The reaction is then
      \begin{equation*} 2\thinspace \text{H}_2 + \thinspace\text{O}_2 \to 2\thinspace \text{H}_2\text{O}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. Proceeding as in the previous part, we have
    \begin{equation*} \begin{alignedat}{8} \text{K:} \amp \thinspace \amp x_1 \amp \amp \amp \amp \amp \amp \amp {}-{} \amp 2x_5 \amp \amp \amp {}={} \amp 0 \\ \text{Mn:} \amp \thinspace \amp x_1 \amp {}+{} \amp x_2 \amp \amp \amp {}-{} \amp x_4 \amp \amp \amp \amp \amp {}={} \amp 0 \\ \text{O:} \amp \thinspace \amp 4x_1 \amp {}+{} \amp 4x_2 \amp {}+{} \amp x_3 \amp {}-{} \amp 2x_4 \amp {}-{} \amp 4x_5 \amp {}-{} \amp 4x_6 \amp {}={} \amp 0 \\ \text{S:} \amp \thinspace \amp \amp \amp x_2 \amp \amp \amp \amp \amp {}-{} \amp x_5 \amp {}-{} \amp x_6 \amp {}={} \amp 0 \\ \text{H:} \amp \thinspace \amp \amp \amp \amp \amp 2x_3 \amp \amp \amp \amp \amp {}-{} \amp 2x_6 \amp {}= \amp 0\text{.} \\ \end{alignedat} \end{equation*}
    Solving this linear system, we find
    \begin{equation*} \begin{alignedat}{2} x_1 \amp {}={} \amp x_6 \\ x_2 \amp {}={} \amp \frac32 x_6 \\ x_3 \amp {}={} \amp x_6 \\ x_4 \amp {}={} \amp \frac52 x_6 \\ x_5 \amp {}={} \amp \frac12 x_6\text{.} \\ \end{alignedat} \end{equation*}
    Once again, we choose \(x_6\) to be the smallest positive integer that causes all of the variables to be integers. This means that \(x_6 = 2\) so that we have
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rrr} x_1 = 2, \amp x_2 = 3, \amp x_3 = 2, \\ x_4 = 5, \amp x_5 = 1, \amp x_6 = 2\text{.} \\ \end{array} \end{equation*}
    This gives the reaction:
    \begin{equation*} 2\thinspace \text{K}\text{Mn}\text{O}_4 + 3\thinspace \text{Mn}\text{S}\text{O}_4 + 2\thinspace \text{H}_2\text{O} \to 5\thinspace \text{Mn}\text{O}_2 + \thinspace \text{K}_2\text{S}\text{O}_4 + 2\thinspace \text{H}_2\text{S}\text{O}_4. \end{equation*}
