Item 3.2.5.8.e.
It is the center of a hexagon. Adding or subtracting \(\twovec{3}{0}\) to the coordinates translates one hexagon to another. This means that \(\coords{\xvec}{\bcal} = \twovec{16}{4}\) can be translated to \(\coords{\xvec}{\bcal} = \twovec{1}{4}\text{,}\) which is the center of a hexagon.
