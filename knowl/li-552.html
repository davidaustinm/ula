Item 2.1.4.5.b.
Here we have
\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{rrr|r} 2 \amp 0 \amp 4 \amp 3 \\ -1 \amp 3 \amp 4 \amp 7 \\ -2 \amp 1 \amp -2 \amp 1 \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{rrr|r} 1 \amp 0 \amp 2 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp 2 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 0 \amp 1 \end{array}\right]\text{,} \end{equation*}
which represents an inconsistent system and shows that \(\bvec\) cannot be written as a linear combination of \(\vvec_1\text{,}\) \(\vvec_2\text{,}\) and \(\vvec_3\text{.}\)
