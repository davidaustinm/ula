1. We have
    \begin{equation*} \twovec{x}{y} = 2\vvec_1 -3\vvec_2 = \twovec{1}{-4}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. We have \(\twovec{2}{-3} = c\vvec_1 + d\vvec_2 \text{.}\) Solving this equation, we find \(c=7/3\) and \(d=-8/3\text{,}\) which means that \((c,d) = \{7/3, -8/3\}\text{.}\)
  3. As in the first part of this problem, we write
    \begin{equation*} \begin{alignedat}{2} x \amp {}={} \amp 2c+d \\ y \amp {}={} \amp c+2d\text{.} \\ \end{alignedat} \end{equation*}
  4. We want to solve \(\twovec{x}{y} = c\vvec_1 + d\vvec_2\) by constructing the augmented matrix and finding its reduced row echelon form.
    \begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{rr|r} 2 \amp 1 \amp x \\ 1 \amp 2 \amp y \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}
