Item c.
Each time we multiply by \(A\text{,}\) the eigenvectors are multiplied by their associated eigenvalues. This gives
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \xvec_1 = A\xvec_0 \amp = 2(1.3)\vvec_1 + 0.5\vvec_2 \\ \xvec_2 = A\xvec_1 \amp = 2(1.3)^2\vvec_1 + 0.5^2\vvec_2 \\ \xvec_3 = A\xvec_2 \amp = 2(1.3)^3\vvec_1 + 0.5^3\vvec_2\text{.} \\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
