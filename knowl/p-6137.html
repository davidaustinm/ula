If we have an orthonormal basis \(\uvec_1,\uvec_2,\ldots,\uvec_n\) for \(W\text{,}\) the projection formula simplifies to
\begin{equation*} \bhat= (\bvec\cdot\uvec_1)~\uvec_1 + (\bvec\cdot\uvec_2)~\uvec_2 + \cdots + (\bvec\cdot\uvec_n)~\uvec_n\text{.} \end{equation*}
If we then form the matrix
\begin{equation*} Q = \begin{bmatrix} \uvec_1 \amp \uvec_2 \amp \ldots \amp \uvec_n \end{bmatrix}\text{,} \end{equation*}
this expression may be succintly written
\begin{align*} \bhat \amp {}={} (\bvec\cdot\uvec_1)~\uvec_1 + (\bvec\cdot\uvec_2)~\uvec_2 + \cdots + (\bvec\cdot\uvec_n)~\uvec_n\\ \amp {}={} \begin{bmatrix} \uvec_1\amp\uvec_2\amp\ldots\amp\uvec_n \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \uvec_1\cdot\bvec \\ \uvec_2\cdot\bvec \\ \vdots \\ \uvec_n\cdot\bvec \\ \end{bmatrix}\\ \amp {}={} QQ^T\bvec \end{align*}
