To determine the distribution of cars on subsequent days, we will repeatedly multiply by \(A\text{.}\) We find that
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \xvec_1 = A\xvec_0 \amp {}={} A(250\vvec_1 - 250\vvec_2) = 250\vvec_1 - (0.2)250\vvec_2 \\ \xvec_2 = A\xvec_1 \amp {}={} A(250\vvec_1 - (0.2)250\vvec_2) = 250\vvec_1 - (0.2)^2250\vvec_2 \\ \xvec_3 = A\xvec_2 \amp {}={} A(250\vvec_1 - (0.2)^2250\vvec_2) = 250\vvec_1 - (0.2)^3250\vvec_2 \\ \xvec_4 = A\xvec_3 \amp {}={} A(250\vvec_1 - (0.2)^3250\vvec_2) = 250\vvec_1 - (0.2)^4250\vvec_2 \\ \xvec_5 = A\xvec_4 \amp {}={} A(250\vvec_1 - (0.2)^4250\vvec_2) = 250\vvec_1 - (0.2)^5250\vvec_2 \\ \end{aligned}\text{.} \end{equation*}
