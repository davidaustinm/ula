Solution 4.2.6.5.1.

  1. Sage tells us there are three distinct eigenvalues \(\lambda=3,2,-1\text{,}\) each having multiplicity one. A basis of eigenvectors is
    \begin{equation*} \left\{\threevec012, \threevec10{-1}, \threevec{-2}{1}{3}\right\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. It is not possible to find a basis of eigenvectors because the eigenvalue \(\lambda=-1\) has multiplicity two but its eigenspace is one-dimensional.
  3. It is possible to find a basis for \(\real^3\) consisting of eignevectors because one eigenvalue \(\lambda=1\) has multiplicity one while the other \(\lambda=-1\) has multiplicity two and a two-dimensional eigenspace. A basis is
    \begin{equation*} \left\{\threevec{-2}32, \threevec10{-1}, \threevec012\right\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
