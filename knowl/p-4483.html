More generally, we have \(\xvec_k=2(1.3)^k\vvec_1 + 0.5^k\vvec_2\text{.}\) As \(k\) grows large, \(0.5^k\) becomes insignificantly small so that \(\xvec_k\approx 2(1.3)^k\vvec_1\text{.}\) This means that \(R_k\approx 4(1.3)^k\) and \(S_k\approx2(1.3)^k\) so that the ratio \(R_k/S_k \approx 2\text{.}\)
