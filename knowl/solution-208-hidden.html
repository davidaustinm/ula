1. We have \(\det A = -3\text{,}\) and the product of the eigenvalues is \(-3\text{.}\)
  2. We have \(\det A = 12\text{,}\) and the product of the eigenvalues is \(12\)
  3. We suspect that \(\det A\) equals the product of the eigenvalues.
  4. Setting \(\lambda=0\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \det(A-0I) \amp {}={} (\lambda_1-0)(\lambda_2-0)\ldots(\lambda_m-0) \\ \det A \amp {}={}\lambda_1\lambda_2\ldots\lambda_n\text{.} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
    Notice that we mean the product of the eigenvalues, including their multiplicities.
