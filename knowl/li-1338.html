Item 2.5.5.5.a.
  1. We have
    \begin{equation*} T(\xvec) = \begin{bmatrix} 10 \amp 20 \\ 50 \amp 30 \\ 30 \amp 30 \\ \end{bmatrix} \twovec{x_1}{x_2} = \threevec{10x_1+20x_2}{50x_1+20x_2}{30x_1+30x_2}. \end{equation*}
  2. The number of cakes produced is \(10x_1 + 20x_2\text{.}\)
  3. This is consistent with the given information because Bakery 1 produces cakes at the rate of 10 cakes per hour so the number of cakes it produces is \(10x_1\text{.}\) In the same way, the number of cakes produced by Bakery 2 is \(20x_2\) giving a total of \(10x_1 + 20x_2\text{.}\)
