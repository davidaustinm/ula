Solution 7.2.4.10.1.

  1. \begin{align*} f_x \amp {}={} 4x^3 - 4yz = 0\\ f_y \amp {}={} 4y^3 - 4xz = 0\\ f_z \amp {}={} 4z^3 - 4xy = 0 \end{align*}
    and all three equations are satisfied at the point \((-1,1,-1)\text{.}\)
    The Hessian matrix at that point is \(H=\begin{bmatrix} 12 \amp 4 \amp -4 \\ 4 \amp 12 \amp 4 \\ -4 \amp 4 \amp 12 \end{bmatrix} \)
  2. The eigenvalues are \(4\text{,}\) \(16\text{,}\) and \(16\) so \(H\) is positive definite
  3. This implies that the critical point is a local minimum.
