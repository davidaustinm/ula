1. If \(A\) and \(B\) are invertible, then \(\det(A) \neq 0\) and \(\det(B) \neq 0\text{.}\) Therefore, \(\det(AB) = \det(A)\det(B) \neq 0\text{,}\) which shows that \(AB\) is invertible.
  2. If \(AB\) is invertible, then \(\det (AB) = \det(A) \det(B) \neq 0\text{.}\) This implies that \(\det(A)\neq0\) and \(\det(B) \neq 0\text{,}\) from which we conclude that both \(A\) and \(B\) are invertible.
