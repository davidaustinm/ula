1. We find the eigenvalues \(\lambda_1=1.1\) with associated eigenvector \(\vvec_1=\twovec11\) and \(\lambda_2=0.8\) with associated eigenvector \(\vvec_2=\twovec21\text{.}\)
  2. After constructing an augmented matrix, we find that \(\xvec_0=\vvec_1+5\vvec_2 \text{.}\)
  3. Since \(A(c_1\vvec_1+c_2\vvec_2) = 1.1c_1\vvec_1 + 0.8c_2\vvec_2\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \xvec_1 \amp {}={} 1.1\vvec_1+0.8\cdot5\vvec_2 \\ \xvec_2 \amp {}={} 1.1^2\vvec_1+0.8^2\cdot5\vvec_2 \\ \xvec_3 \amp {}={} 1.1^3\vvec_1+0.8^3\cdot5\vvec_2 \\ \end{aligned}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  4. We have \(\xvec_k = 1.1^k\vvec_1+0.8^k\cdot5\vvec_2\text{.}\) As \(k\) grows larger, \(0.8^k\) becomes less significant. Eventually, \(\xvec_k \approx 1.1^k\vvec_1= \twovec{1.1^k}{1.1^k}\text{.}\)
