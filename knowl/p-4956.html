False. If \(A\) is an invertible stochastic matrix, the eigenvalues of \(A\) must satisfy \(0\lt |\lambda_j| \leq 1\text{.}\) The eigenvalues of \(A^{-1}\) are \(\frac1{\lambda_j}\text{,}\) which satisfy \(1\leq \left|\frac1{\lambda_j}\right|\) so \(A^{-1}\) is most likely not stochastic.
