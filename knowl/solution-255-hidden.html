1. Notice that both \(A\) and \(I\) are stochastic. Therefore, \(S(A-I)=SA-SI = S-S = \left[\begin{array}{rrrr}0\amp0\amp\cdots\amp 0 \end{array}\right]\text{.}\)
  2. \(S\evec_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \end{bmatrix}\text{.}\)
  3. We have
    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} (A-I)\xvec \amp = \evec_1 \\ S(A-I)\xvec \amp = S\evec_1 \\ \begin{bmatrix} 0 \end{bmatrix} \amp = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \end{bmatrix}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
    This says that the original equation \((A-I)\xvec = \evec_1\) is not consistent.
  4. The span of the columns of \(A-I\) is not \(\real^n\text{.}\)
  5. Because the span of the columns of \(A-I\) is not \(\real^n\text{,}\) \(A-I\) has a row without a pivot position, which says that \(A-I\) is not invertible. Therefore, \(\det(A-I) = 0\text{,}\) which says that \(\lambda = 1\) is an eigenvalue of \(A\text{.}\)
