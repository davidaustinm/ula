There are two simple algebraic operations we often perform on vectors.
Scalar Multiplication
We multiply a vector \(\vvec\) by a real number \(c\) by multiplying each of the components of \(\vvec\) by \(c\text{.}\) For instance,
\begin{equation*} -3\left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{r} -6 \\ 12 \\ -3 \\ \end{array}\right]. \end{equation*}
We will frequently refer to real numbers, such as -3 in this example, as scalars to distinguish them from vectors.
Vector Addition
We add two vectors of the same dimension by adding their components. For instance,
\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{r} 2 \\ -4 \\ 3 \\ \end{array}\right] + \left[\begin{array}{r} -5 \\ 6 \\ -3 \\ \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ \end{array}\right]. \end{equation*}
