Because all the entries in both \(A\) and \(\xvec\) are nonnegative, it follows that the components of \(A\xvec\) are nonnegative. Then we have \(S(A\xvec)=(SA)\xvec = S\xvec = [1]\text{,}\) which shows that the components of \(\xvec\) add to \(1\text{.}\) Therefore, \(\xvec\) is a probability vector.
