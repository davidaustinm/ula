If we have another basis \(\ccal\text{,}\) we find, in the same way, that \(\xvec = P_{\ccal}\coords{\xvec}{\ccal}\) for the conversion between coordinates in the basis \(\ccal\) into standard coordinates. We then have
\begin{equation*} \coords{\xvec}{\bcal} = P^{-1}_{\bcal} \xvec = P_{\bcal}^{-1}(P_{\ccal} \coords{\xvec}{\ccal}) = (P_{\bcal}^{-1}P_{\ccal}) \coords{\xvec}{\ccal}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Therefore, \(P_{\bcal}^{-1}P_{\ccal}\) is the matrix that converts \(\ccal\)-coordinates into \(\bcal\)-coordinates.
