Activity 7.1.2.

Remember that the Sage command A.right_eigenmatrix() attempts to find a basis for \(\real^m\) consisting of eigenvectors of \(A\text{.}\) In particular, the assignment D, P = A.right_eigenmatrix() provides a diagonal matrix \(D\) constructed from the eigenvalues of \(A\) with the columns of \(P\) containing the associated eigenvectors.
  1. For each of the following matrices, determine whether there is a basis for \(\real^2\) consisting of eigenvectors of that matrix. When there is such a basis, form the matrices \(P\) and \(D\) and verify that the matrix equals \(PDP^{-1}\text{.}\)
    1. \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 \amp -4 \\ 4 \amp 3 \end{bmatrix} \text{.}\)
    2. \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 1 \\ -1 \amp 3 \end{bmatrix} \text{.}\)
    3. \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 0\\ -1 \amp 2 \end{bmatrix} \text{.}\)
    4. \(\begin{bmatrix} 9 \amp 2 \\ 2 \amp 6 \end{bmatrix} \text{.}\)
  2. For which of these examples is it possible to form an orthogonal basis for \(\real^2\) consisting of eigenvectors?
  3. For any such matrix \(A\text{,}\) find an orthonormal basis of eigenvectors and explain why \(A=QDQ^{-1}\) where \(Q\) is an orthogonal matrix.
  4. Finally, explain why \(A=QDQ^T\) in this case.
  5. When \(A=QDQ^T\text{,}\) what is the relationship between \(A\) and \(A^T\text{?}\)
