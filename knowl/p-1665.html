Yes. If \(c_1\vvec_1 + c_2\vvec_2 = \zerovec\) and one of the weights is not zero, we can rearrange this expression to solve for one of the vectors as a scalar multiple of the other. For instance, if \(2\vvec_1-3\vvec_2=\zerovec\text{,}\) then \(\vvec_1 = \frac32\vvec_2\text{.}\)
