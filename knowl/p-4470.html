We will record the populations in a vector \(\xvec_k = \twovec{R_k}{S_k}\) and note that \(\xvec_{k+1} = A\xvec_k\) where \(A = \left[\begin{array}{rr} 0.9 \amp 0.8 \\ 0.2 \amp 0.9 \\ \end{array}\right] \text{.}\)
  1. Verify that
    \begin{equation*} \vvec_1=\twovec{2}{1},\qquad \vvec_2=\twovec{-2}{1} \end{equation*}
    are eigenvectors of \(A\) and find their respective eigenvalues.
  2. Suppose that initially \(\xvec_0 = \twovec{2}{3}\text{.}\) Write \(\xvec_0\) as a linear combination of the eigenvectors \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\text{.}\)
  3. Write the vectors \(\xvec_1\text{,}\) \(\xvec_2\text{,}\) and \(\xvec_3\) as linear combinations of the eigenvectors \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\text{.}\)
  4. What happens to \(\xvec_k\) after a very long time?
  5. When \(k\) becomes very large, what happens to the ratio of the populations \(R_k/S_k\text{?}\)
  6. After a very long time, by approximately what factor does the population of \(R\) grow every year? By approximately what factor does the population of \(S\) grow every year?
  7. If we begin instead with \(\xvec_0 = \twovec{4}{4}\text{,}\) what eventually happens to the ratio \(R_k/S_k\) as \(k\) becomes very large?
