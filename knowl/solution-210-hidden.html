1. We have eigenvalues \(\lambda_1=1\) with associated eigenvector \(\vvec_1=\twovec12\) and \(\lambda_2=0.1\) with associated eigenvector \(\vvec_2=\twovec{-1}{1}\text{.}\)
  2. After constructing an augmented matrix, we find \(\xvec_0=\frac13\vvec_1+\frac13\vvec_2\text{.}\)
  3. Since \(A(c_1\vvec_1+c_2\vvec_2) = c_1\vvec_1 + 0.1c_2\vvec_2\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \xvec_1 \amp {}={} \frac13\vvec_1+0.1\frac13\vvec_2 \\ \xvec_2 \amp {}={} \frac13\vvec_1+0.1^2\frac13\vvec_2 \\ \xvec_3 \amp {}={} \frac13\vvec_1+0.1^3\frac13\vvec_2. \\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
  4. We have \(\xvec_k = \frac13\vvec_1+0.1^k\frac13\vvec_2\text{.}\) As \(k\) grows larger, \(0.1^k\) becomes less significant. Eventually, \(\xvec_k \approx \frac13\vvec_1= \twovec{\frac13}{\frac23}\text{.}\)
