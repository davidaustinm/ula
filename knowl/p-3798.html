If \(\vvec\) is an eigenvector of \(A\) with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda\text{,}\) then \(A\vvec=\lambda\vvec\text{.}\) Therefore, \(A^2\vvec = A(A\vvec) = \lambda A\vvec = \lambda^2\vvec\text{,}\) which means that \(\vvec\) is an eigenvector with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda^2\text{.}\)
