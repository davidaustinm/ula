With a birth rate of \(80\)%, the first eigenvalue goes up to \(\lambda_1=1.02\text{,}\) which means that, eventually, all the populations grow by \(2\)%. An eigenvector is approximately \(\vvec_1 = \threevec{78}{69}{100}\) so there are about \(78\) juveniles and \(69\) yearlings for every \(100\) adults.
