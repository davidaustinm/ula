We have
\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{rr|r} 2 \amp 4 \amp 3 \\ -1 \amp k \amp l \\ \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{rr|r} 1 \amp 2 \amp \frac32 \\ 0 \amp 2+k \amp l + \frac32 \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}
The system is inconsistent if \(k=-2\) and \(l\neq -\frac32\text{.}\) There are infinitely many solutions if \(k=-2\) and \(l=-\frac32\text{.}\) There is a unique solution if \(k\neq -2\text{.}\)
