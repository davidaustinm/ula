Since the columns of \(P_{\bcal}\) are the basis vectors \(\vvec_1,\vvec_2,\ldots,\vvec_m\text{,}\) we know that \(P_{\bcal} \sim I_m\text{,}\) and \(P_{\bcal}\) is therefore invertible. Since we have
\begin{equation*} \xvec = P_{\bcal}\coords{\xvec}{\bcal}\text{,} \end{equation*}
we must also have
\begin{equation*} P_{\bcal}^{-1}\xvec = \coords{\xvec}{\bcal}\text{.} \end{equation*}
