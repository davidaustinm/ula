  2. Then we have \(\xvec_n = A^n\xvec_0 = \lambda_1^n\vvec_1 + \lambda_2^n\vvec_2\text{.}\) Since \(L_n\) is the second component of \(\xvec_n\text{,}\) we have \(L_n = \lambda_1^n + \lambda_2^n\text{.}\)
  3. Because \(|\lambda_2| \lt 1\text{,}\) larger powers \(\lambda_2^n\) are very close to zero. Therefore, \(L_n\approx \lambda_1^n = \left(\frac{1+\sqrt5}{2}\right)^n\text{.}\)
