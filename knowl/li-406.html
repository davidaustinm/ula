Item 1.4.4.6.a.
We have
\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{rr|r} -1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \\ 2 \amp -4 \amp k \\ \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{rr|r} 1 \amp -2 \amp -3 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp k+6 \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}
This shows that the system is consistent when \(k= -6\text{,}\) and the solution can never be unique. If \(k\neq -6\text{,}\) the system is inconsistent.
