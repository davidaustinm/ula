Solution 6.4.4.7.1.

  1. \(QQ^T\) is \(7\times7\) and projects vectors orthogonally onto \(\col(A)\text{.}\)
  2. \(Q^TQ\) is the \(4\times4\) identity matrix because the product computes dot products between the columns of \(Q\text{.}\)
  3. \(R\) is a \(4\times4\) upper triangular matrix.
  4. The columns of \(A\) form an orthogonal set.
