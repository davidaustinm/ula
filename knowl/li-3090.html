Item b.
Now we have
\begin{equation*} \begin{alignedat}{3} R_{k+1} \amp {}={} \amp 0.7R_k \amp{}+{} \amp 0.3S_k\\ S_{k+1} \amp {}={} \amp \amp \amp 1.6S_k\text{.} \\ \end{alignedat} \end{equation*}
The population of species \(S\) is not influenced by species \(R\text{.}\) However, we see that the population of species \(R\) grows in the presence of species \(S\text{.}\) In other words, species \(S\) helps species \(R\) to grow so we say that \(S\) is beneficial for \(R\text{.}\)
Because the eigenvalues are \(\lambda_1=0.7\) and \(\lambda_2=1.6\text{,}\) this dynamical system is again a saddle. The associated eigenvectors are \(\vvec_1=\twovec10\) and \(\vvec_2=\twovec13\text{.}\) After a long time, the population vector \(\xvec_k\approx c_1(1.6)^k\vvec_2\) so both populations grow by a factor of 1.6 and with a ratio \(R_k/S_k=1/3.\)
