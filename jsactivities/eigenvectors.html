Eigenvectors

An eigenvector of a square matrix $A$ is a nonzero vector ${\mathbf v}$ such that $A{\mathbf v} = \lambda {\mathbf v}$, where the scalar $\lambda$ is the associated eigenvalue.

The sliders in the diagram below allow you to choose a matrix \(A=\begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}\). The vector \({\mathbf v}\), which is shaded red, may be moved by clicking in the head of the vector. The vector \(A{\mathbf v}\) is then shown in outline.
